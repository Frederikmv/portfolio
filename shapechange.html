The Divider: Exploring How Shape- Changing Objects can Create Work Environments in Classrooms

Abstract

Over the years, schools have experienced an increasing amount of pupils which causes challenges in utilizing room capacity and space. This also results in a lack of privacy and work environments for groups during breaks and lessons. In this pictorial, we present the Divider: a shape-changing room divider for the classroom, that aims to create a better work environment for pupils by offering a degree of privacy within the space created. We further present the process of designing and developing the concept of the Divider as well as low- and high-fidelity prototypes to elucidate and visualize the change in shape, interaction and use. Also, we discuss some of the limitations and aspects of the Divider.

Timeframe: 5 month course

My role:

In this project I was mostly responsible for interviewing and doing workshops with children in a local school and creating the prototype. Creating the prototype consisted of working with wood, both lasercutting and planing. Furthermore, I created the 3D models showing the design of The Divider.

The Divider

This is a 3D model showcasing how the final design of the Divider should look. The two areas with perforated fabric can expand and contract to to open or close creating a more public or private space.

Inteaction

Two sliders alongside the edge activates to linear actuators that then expand or contract the Divider.

Perforated fabric

We experimented with many different patterns of perforation before finding a pattern that could be expanded and contracted several times without making the fabric loose its strength.
